What is an insurance policy limit?

An insurance policy limit is the maximum amount your insurance company will pay for your injury or illness. Most personal injury protection (PIP) policies have an annual cap on the amount of coverage. The amount of coverage you have determines how much you will receive if you file a claim. If you have a high limit and are injured in an accident, you might receive the entire policy limit. However, if you have a low limit and are injured in the same accident, you might receive only a portion of your coverage. If you have a high limit but are injured in a low-impact accident, you could receive more than you need. On the other hand, if you have a low limit and are injured in a high-impact accident, you might receive less than you need.
