What is an insurance policy rider?

Riders are optional provisions that can be added to your insurance policy. They may be related to specific types of coverage or how much your policy will pay in specific situations. Riders help you tailor your policy to meet your specific needs. That makes them particularly useful if you already have a policy that covers most risks, but want to add coverage for something specific. They’re also a good option if you have a special circumstance that makes you more likely to file a claim. In those cases, having a rider in place will help protect you from having to pay higher premiums as a result. Read on to learn more about riders and how to find the right one for your needs.

What is a rider?

A rider is a provision that is added to a policy’s main coverage. It’s an optional addition that can expand or contract coverage. Riders can be customized to meet the specific needs of an individual policyholder. They can also be used to address special circumstances or changes in the insurance market.
