How does life insurance work?

Working with a broker is one of the best ways to get life insurance that fits your budget. Brokers can shop multiple companies and types of policies to find you the best option based on your needs and budget. There are a variety of different types of life insurance available to suit different needs. Some policies are designed to provide a lump sum payment to your beneficiaries if you die; others offer regular payments to cover living costs for a set period. Here's how life insurance works in general terms:

What is life insurance?

It's a form of insurance that provides financial protection if you die before your policy's end date. There are different types of life insurance that each has its own purpose and use.Term insurance: Term insurance is a type of life insurance that provides a fixed amount of money at a fixed rate of payment for a specified period of time. For example, you might want a $100,000 term policy that pays you $1,000 per month for 10 years. The policy would then end, and you would receive a final payout of the remaining balance.Whole life insurance: Whole life insurance is a type of permanent insurance that builds cash value over time. You make regular payments into the policy, and the amount of coverage grows as the cash value increases.

How does permanent life insurance work?

Permanent life insurance is a type of policy that builds cash value over time. As you pay premiums, the insurance company invests those funds and pays out a portion of those earnings as insurance coverage. The amount of coverage is based on your age and health at the time the policy is issued.Term life insurance: Term life insurance is a type of insurance that provides a set amount of money at a fixed rate of payment for a specified period of time. For example, you might want a $100,000 term policy that pays you $1,000 per month for 10 years. The policy would then end, and you would receive a final payout of the remaining balance.Whole life insurance: Whole life insurance is a type of permanent insurance that builds cash value over time. How does term life insurance work?

How does universal life insurance work?

Universal life insurance is a type of policy that provides a combination of both a death benefit and an investment component. The death benefit portion is similar to a term life insurance policy, while the investment portion grows over time. The death benefit portion of the policy is the amount that would be paid out if you died before the end of the term or the policy's expiration. The investment portion is the amount that would be paid out if you lived longer than the term or policy expiration. Universal life insurance policies are flexible because you can choose how you want to use the money in the account. You can invest in stocks, bonds, or a combination of both to get the best return on your money.

How does a cash value life insurance policy work?

Cash value life insurance policies are a type of permanent life insurance policy that provides a combination of protection and a cash value. The cash value portion grows over time as you make premium payments and can be used to pay a portion of your final expenses if you die before the policy expires. The death benefit portion is a set amount of money that would be paid out if you died before the policy expires.Cash value life insurance policies are flexible because you can choose how you want to use the money in the account. You can invest in stocks, bonds, or a combination of both to get the best return on your money.

Final words

There are many different types of life insurance policies available, and it's important to understand the different benefits and features of each type. The best way to do that is to meet with a broker to shop different options. Brokers can assist you in finding the right policy for your needs and budget. Life insurance is an important part of a financial plan, and the right policy can help ensure your family is protected if anything happens to you.
