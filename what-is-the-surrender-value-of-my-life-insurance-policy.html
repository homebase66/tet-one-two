What is the surrender value of my life insurance policy?

When you buy life insurance, the cost is almost always more than the coverage. If you’re buying term insurance to protect your retirement savings or for a mortgage payment, the cost of coverage is usually affordable. But if you want to buy a policy to protect your loved ones financially if something happens to you, the cost can be much higher. The surrender value is the amount your policy will pay out if you decide to terminate your coverage before the end date. This article will explain what the surrender value of your policy is and why it’s important to understand before deciding on the best coverage for you and your family.

What is the surrender value?

The surrender value is the amount your life insurance policy will pay out if you decide to terminate your coverage before the end date. Your policy may state that you can request a specific amount when you want to terminate your coverage. Or it may state that you can terminate your coverage any time and request a specific amount at that time. If you decide to terminate your insurance policy before the end date, you will receive a check for the amount that your policy will pay out.If you decide to terminate your policy early, you will no longer have the opportunity to increase the death benefit. You will also lose any dividends or cash values that your policy had built up. If you decide to terminate your policy, you will lose any investment gains you had made.

Why is the surrender value important?

If you are buying a life insurance policy to protect your loved ones financially if something happens to you, the amount of coverage you choose will be based on your current financial situation. You may be able to get a lower cost if you let the policy expire before the end date. However, if you decide to terminate the policy early, the amount you receive will be less than the amount you paid.The lower the amount you receive, the less coverage your loved ones will receive. That’s why it’s important to understand the surrender value of your policy before you decide on the best coverage for you and your family.
